Biography

I got started in design mostly out of curiosity. I had a couple hobbies that I enjoyed making websites for and I was learning a lot about digital interfaces and HCI in order to improve my sites. As my knowledge in that field grew, I became more invested in learning as much as I could about design, mostly from a web design perspective.

In my last semester of college, studying Fine Arts: Design, I secured a job as a web designer for an agency that has since been absorbed. We had a project come in to build a learning management system and that’s when I discovered UI design and really started putting names to the concepts that make up UX design. I found my stride here working on this application and the partner company took notice and I was asked to join as the lead designer for a race event management software. I was working across a lot of the product life cycle at this stage, even teaching myself to code, becoming responsible for all discovery, documentation, design, and implementation of the front end. After this, I joined EAGLE6. I have been with EAGLE6 for nearly 5 years, starting in June of 2015 as a UX researcher focusing on usability, information design & architecture, and user modeling.
